Research Associate · SUTD Singapore

Harshit Bokadia

Embodied AI & World Models  ·  Probabilistic Reasoning  ·  Interpretability

I build AI systems that reason about physical behaviour — learning internal representations of how agents move, explore, and adapt in the world. At SUTD (Singapore, MoE-funded), I design Bayesian inverse-RL pipelines that infer learners' world models from high-dimensional kinematic trajectories and scaffold open-ended embodied creativity in real time.

My path here is deliberately cross-disciplinary: mechanical engineering gave me a systems instinct for physical dynamics; industrial & systems engineering and cognitive science at Rutgers opened the door to neural computation and clinical AI; and applied work at Canadian hospitals — on biosignals, neurodivergence, and precision health — sharpened my ability to build models that hold up against messy, real-world data. I am now oriented toward the intersection of physical AI, embodied agents, and world model learning, with a parallel interest in mechanistic interpretability and alignment for LLMs and VLMs — work I connect directly to my DARPA-adjacent XAI research and vector-symbolic architecture contributions.

I am currently pursuing an MS in AI at UT Austin (remote/part-time) and am actively seeking a thesis advisor and research collaborations in these areas.

Embodied AI · World Models · Physical AI

SUTD / MoE SoL Grant · 2026–ongoing

AI-Driven Scaffolding of Embodied Creativity

End-to-end pipeline for real-time estimation of learners' internal world models from motion-capture kinematic data. We model human movement exploration as a Markov Decision Process, run Bayesian inverse RL to recover reward structure and state-transition dynamics, and dynamically scaffold open-ended creativity. MuJoCo humanoid simulation grounds model learning in physically plausible motor behaviour.

Network-theoretic analysis of EEG-derived neural connectivity as participants learn novel motor tasks with and without proprioceptive feedback. Encoding Medical Ontologies with Holographic Reduced Representations for Transformers

Introduces holographic reduced representations (HRRs) — a class of vector-symbolic architectures — as a structured, binding-preserving way to encode medical ontologies into transformer token spaces. Demonstrates improved knowledge retention and compositional generalization in clinical NLP tasks. Directly relevant to knowledge-infused LLM pretraining, structured post-training, and efficient inference with external knowledge.

XAI · Interpretability · Medical Imaging

Applied AI Letters · 2022 · DOI: 10.1002/ail2.77 · 13 citations

Evaluating Perceptual and Semantic Interpretability of Saliency Methods: A Case Study of Melanoma

DARPA XAI-adjacent work. Designed two novel evaluation metrics for saliency-based explanations: one measuring perceptual coherence of attribution maps, another comparing maps against dermatologist-defined semantic features. Benchmarked six saliency methods on VGG-16 across ISIC data distributions. Enables adaptive method selection for high-stakes clinical AI — and provides a principled framework transferable to mechanistic interpretability of LLMs and VLMs.

Projects

Embodied AI · World Models 2026 – present

Embodied Creativity Pipeline · SUTD

Problem: How do we measure and scaffold open-ended creativity in physical movement — and can we build an AI that infers a learner's internal world model in real time?

My role: Lead engineer on the full AI pipeline. I implemented MuJoCo humanoid simulation for physically plausible trajectory generation, time-delay embedding for kinematic state-space construction, Bayesian inverse RL (GAIL-family) to recover reward functions and transition dynamics from motion capture data, and UMAP-based movement cluster visualization for real-time scaffolding feedback.

Methods: Bayesian inverse RL · MDP formulation · MuJoCo · UMAP · wearable physiological sensors (pupil kinematics, GSR, HR)

Connection: Directly addresses open-ended learning — a frontier problem shared by embodied AI, model-based RL, and developmental robotics.

LLMs · Vector-Symbolic Architectures 2023 – 2024

Holographic Reduced Representations for Transformers · Waterloo

Problem: Transformers struggle to encode relational and hierarchical medical ontologies in a structure-preserving way during pretraining.

My role: Co-designed the HRR encoding scheme, ran experiments on medical ontology injection into transformer token spaces, and contributed to evaluation across clinical NLP benchmarks.

Methods: Holographic Reduced Representations (VSA family) · PyTorch · UMLS medical ontologies · transformer fine-tuning

Connection: Grounds my interest in structured knowledge in LLMs — relevant to post-training, efficient inference, and multimodal grounding for VLMs.

XAI · Mechanistic Interpretability 2020 – 2022

Saliency Evaluation Framework for Clinical AI · Rutgers / DARPA XAI

Problem: Existing saliency methods for medical imaging lacked rigorous, clinically grounded evaluation metrics — making it impossible to select methods for high-stakes deployment.

My role: First author. Designed the full evaluation framework, implemented two novel metrics (perceptual coherence + semantic alignment to expert features), ran comparative benchmarks across six saliency methods on a VGG-16 melanoma classifier.

Methods: PyTorch · VGG-16 · ISIC dataset · GradCAM · LIME · SHAP · custom perceptual/semantic metrics

Connection: The evaluation framework I designed has a direct analogue in mechanistic interpretability for LLMs — where we similarly need principled metrics for attribution faithfulness and semantic alignment.

Clinical AI · Mentorship 2024 – 2025

Sociodemographic Bias in Adaptive Functioning · Holland Bloorview

Problem: Standardized adaptive functioning assessments used in clinical decisions for neurodivergent children are systematically biased by socioeconomic status, sex, and ethnicity.

My role: Mentor to intern Zuhair Qureshi (McMaster). Guided study design, feature engineering from the POND Network dataset (n=1,254), XGBoost modeling with 15-fold cross-validation, bias analysis via Mann-Whitney testing across demographic subgroups, and disparity ratio visualization. Work presented as a research poster.

Methods: XGBoost · Mann-Whitney testing · Feature importance · Parity ratios · POND Network dataset

Impact: Identified statistically significant bias across all demographic groupings — informing precision health and bias-aware clinical tools for pediatric populations.

Computational Neuroscience · Biosignals 2018 – 2020

Neural Connectivity & Digitized ADOS · Rutgers Cognitive Science

Problem: Clinical observation of motor and social behaviour in autism misses sub-second, high-dimensional dynamics that may carry diagnostic signal.

My role: First author on two papers. Applied EEG signal processing and network-theoretic analysis (graph measures of neural connectivity) to motor learning paradigms; separately applied wearable biosensors to ADOS sessions to extract kinematic features from socio-motor dyads.

Methods: EEG · EEGLAB · MATLAB · Graph theory (network neuroscience) · Wearable inertial sensors · Time-series analysis

Connection: This is the empirical root of my interest in embodied AI — physical behaviour as a window into internal states — that now drives my world model work at SUTD.
